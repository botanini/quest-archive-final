D&D Starter Kit

New to Dungeons & Dragons?

This starter kit is here to help you take your first steps into the world of adventuring.

Whether you're building your first character or preparing to DM a one-shot, we've gathered essential tools, guides, and tips to get you going without overwhelmed.

Here's what every beginner should have in their pack.

Essential Supplies

  • Set of Dice: A standard 7-piece polyhedral set (d20, d12, d10, d8, d6, d4, and percentile).
  • Character Sheet: Paper or digital form to track stats, inventory, spells, and traits.
  • Pencil: A humble yet powerful tool for edits and erasures during play.
  • Player’s Handbook (PHB): Physical or digital version; even a free basic rules PDF works!
  • D&D Beyond Account: Recommended for digital character creation and rules lookup. Visit D&D Beyond.

Helpful Resources

  • Intro to Classes: Unsure who to play? Try a Fighter for simplicity or a Cleric for flexibility.
  • Session Zero Checklist: Establish expectations, tone, and rules with your group before play.
  • How Combat Works: Learn initiative, actions, and turns to keep battles smooth.
  • Roleplaying Tips: Speak in character, ask questions, and be curious — not perfect!
  • One-Shot Module: Try a prewritten short adventure like “The Wild Sheep Chase” or “Frozen Sick.”

What to Expect in Your First Game

Your first session might be chaotic — and that’s okay! Expect laughter, weird dice rolls, and creative problem solving.

Don’t worry about mastering the rules immediately; focus on enjoying the story and contributing to the fun. D&D is about collaborative storytelling, and your imagination is the most important item in your kit.
