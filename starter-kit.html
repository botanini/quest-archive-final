D&D Starter Kit

New to Dungeons & Dragons?

Welcome, adventurer! If you're brand new to Dungeons & Dragons, this guide will equip you with everything you need to get started. D&D is a tabletop roleplaying game where your decisions shape the story — and chaos is half the fun.

Whether you want to create your first character, join a friend's campaign, or even run a one-shot as Dungeon Master, this starter kit breaks down the essentials with easy-to-follow tips and links to helpful resources.

No need to memorize all the rules — just bring your imagination. Here’s what every beginner should have in their adventurer’s pack.

What to Expect in Your First Game

Your first session will likely be full of questions, jokes, and moments of delightful chaos. Don’t worry if you don’t know every rule — D&D is about storytelling, not perfection.

Sessions usually start with a recap and evolve into player-driven exploration, problem solving, and combat. Be open-minded, help your fellow party members, and most importantly — have fun.

Many groups begin with a free ruleset, so you don’t need to buy anything to start!

Essential Supplies

  • Set of Dice: A standard 7-piece polyhedral set is required. You can grab a physical set from local game stores or use a free online dice roller.
  • Character Sheet: Use the official PDF or go digital with D&D Beyond.
  • Pencil or Digital Notes: You’ll need to track damage, inventory, and ideas. Erasable tools are ideal!
  • Player’s Handbook (PHB): The core rulebook — but you can start with the free Basic Rules PDF if needed.
  • D&D Beyond Account: Highly recommended for free rules access, digital dice, and homebrew tools. Create your account here.

Helpful Resources

  • Intro to Classes: Not sure who to play? Read class descriptions. Fighters are simple, Bards are stylish, Clerics are flexible.
  • Session Zero Checklist: Before playing, discuss tone, rules, character goals, and comfort zones. Use this post to find out how to run a session 0.
  • How Combat Works: Learn the flow of initiative, movement, actions, and reactions. This combat breakdown helps.
  • Roleplaying Tips: You don’t have to act or do voices! Just focus on making choices your character would. This free roleplaying guide has tips.
  • Beginner One-Shot: Start with a short, self-contained adventure like Get The Wild Sheep Chase Here for free or Frozen Sick Here.

For New Dungeon Masters

Thinking of running a game? Brave soul. Here's what helps most:

  • Start Small: Choose a one-shot or a short dungeon crawl. This is a great way to learn the ropes and get comfortable with the rules.
  • Use a Pre-Made Map: Sites like Donjon or 2-Minute Tabletop have free map generators.
  • Use Tools: Try a virtual tabletop like Roll20 or Owlbear Rodeo.
  • Keep it Loose: Don’t over-prepare. Players will surprise you, and improv is part of the fun! Let somethings slide.
